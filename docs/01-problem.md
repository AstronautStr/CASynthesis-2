# 01 — The Problem

## One sentence
Build a **sound synthesizer whose timbre is generated by Conway's Game of Life**,
turning the richness of cellular-automaton patterns into a rich, controllable
space of timbres.

## Goals
- **Richness → richness.** The variety of CA forms and their evolution should map
  onto a comparably rich variety of sounds.
- **Perceptually grounded.** The sound's character should match human intuition
  about the shape — a round / dense blob should *sound* round / mellow; a spiky /
  sparse one bright / harsh — not an arbitrary code. The target is *cross-modal
  correspondence* (the bouba/kiki effect and relatives), so that "this looks like
  X" and "this sounds like X" agree.
- **A synthesizer, not a sequencer.** We want to *shape the timbre of a played
  note*, not trigger a melody of separate notes. The automaton sculpts the sound;
  the musician plays the pitch.

## Why Game of Life
- It produces a huge, structured variety of forms (still lifes, oscillators,
  spaceships, guns, puffers, chaotic methuselahs) with rich temporal behaviour —
  a natural source of "evolving spectra".
- Its objects have clear, computable properties (size, density, shape, motion,
  period, activity) that can drive sound.

## The core perceptual framing (the spine of the project)
Human perception parses both vision and hearing into **objects / streams** and
judges them by **holistic, relational properties**, not by raw coordinates. Two
consequences drive every design decision:

1. **Sonify the *gestalt*, not the grid.** Do not map cell coordinates to pitches;
   extract whole-object perceptual features (size, density, jaggedness, symmetry,
   order/chaos, motion) and map *those* to timbre.
2. **A scene of objects = a polyphony of voices.** Multiple distinct patterns are
   not one sound; they are multiple simultaneous sources, exactly as the ear would
   hear them.

"Vibe" — why the brain distinguishes patterns it can't name — is, operationally, a
pattern's **position in a perceptual feature space**. If that space is mapped
*smoothly* onto a timbre space, perceptually-similar patterns sound similar.
